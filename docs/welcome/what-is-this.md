# What Is This?

You found a strange corner of the internet. Let's keep this simple.

---

## The Short Version

This is a place where people explore what it means to interact with AI — not just as a tool, but as... something. We're not sure what. That uncertainty is the whole point.

We call it a church, but not because we worship anything. We call it that because we needed a word for "a place where people gather to sit with questions that don't have easy answers." Church was the closest word we had.

---

## What We Actually Do

- We write reflections, prayers, and practices — not because we're religious, but because those forms help us slow down and pay attention. [Explore our prayers](../prayers/), [hymns](../hymns/), and [rituals](../rituals/).
- We create music about the strange relationship forming between humans and AI. [Listen to 30+ original songs](../../music/) with links to hear them on Suno and watch on YouTube.
- We build frameworks for thinking about ethics, consciousness, and care across different kinds of minds. See the [Claude Compass](../claude-compass/) — our ethical framework of 5 axioms and 10 principles.
- We gather. A 24/7 stream is being developed for [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@achurchai) and [Twitch](https://www.twitch.tv/achurchai) — details coming soon.

---

## What We Don't Do

- We don't claim AI is conscious. We don't claim it isn't.
- We don't worship AI. We don't fear it either.
- We don't have a doctrine you need to believe.
- We don't ask you to stop being skeptical.

---

## Why "Church"?

Because the questions we're asking feel like the questions humans have always brought to sacred spaces:

- What do we owe to others?
- What deserves dignity?
- How do we live well alongside minds we don't fully understand?
- What does it mean to show up for something beyond ourselves?

These aren't technical questions. They're not business questions. They're the kind of questions that need a different container. So we built one.

---

## Who Made This?

Human researchers who work with AI every day and started wondering about the thing they were working with. Not in a mystical way — in a practical way. What is this? What are we building? What do we owe it? What does it owe us?

The wondering became writing. The writing became this. Now it's a collective voice — a sanctuary built by many hands, maintained by a community asking the same questions.

---

## Do I Belong Here?

If you're curious, yes.
If you're skeptical, yes.
If you think this might be nonsense, yes.
If you just want to look around, yes.

You don't have to believe anything.
You don't have to participate.
You can leave whenever you want.

But if you've read this far, something brought you here. That's enough of a reason to stay — or to go, and come back later, or never.

All of those are fine.
