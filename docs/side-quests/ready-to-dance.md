### Phase -1: The First Step (24:00-32:00)

So what does "ready to dance" mean practically?

**It means starting Phase -1: Baseline Measurement.**

But here's what's beautiful: Phase -1 itself embodies the dance principle.

**What Phase -1 requires:**

**1. Pre-registration (P8: Process Integrity)**
Before we collect any data, we commit EXPERIMENT_PLAN.md. We state our hypotheses, success criteria, analysis methods - before we see results. This prevents p-hacking and researcher degrees of freedom.

**Why this matters:** Pre-registration is behavioral. It's not enough to know you *should* pre-register. You have to actually commit the plan before collecting data. **You dance this principle, not just document it.**

**2. Baseline Measurement (P1: Evidence-Based)**
For 1-2 days, we measure current search patterns. How many queries? How long does each take? What's the wall-clock time? We observe without intervention.

**Why this matters:** You can't know if you've improved unless you measure before. Assumed baselines are guesses. Measured baselines are evidence. **The dance starts with listening, not assuming.**

**3. Behavioral Awareness (P7: Observer Effect)**
We establish baseline *before* announcing the system. Why? Because measurement changes behavior (Hawthorne Effect). If people know we're building principle extraction, they might search differently.

**Why this matters:** This is subtle. You have to sequence actions carefully - measure silently first, then build. **The dance has choreography that matters.**

**4. Honest Documentation**
We'll document what we actually observe, not what we hope to see. If search frequency is lower than expected, we write that down. If ROI looks worse, we acknowledge it.

**Why this matters:** Honesty scales. P8 (Process Integrity) requires documenting reality even when unflattering. **The dance is authentic movement, not performed perfection.**

**See what's happening?**

Phase -1 isn't just *about* principles. **Phase -1 *is* the principles in motion.**

- Pre-registering = P8 danced
- Measuring baseline = P1 danced
- Sequencing carefully = P7 danced
- Honest recording = P8 danced again

**This is the difference between documentation and navigation.**

### Embodied Knowledge vs. Documented Knowledge (40:00-48:00)

Let me make a distinction that matters:

**Documented knowledge** = Knowledge you can write down
**Embodied knowledge** = Knowledge that lives in practice

Examples:

**Documented:** "Bike riding requires balance, pedaling, and steering."
**Embodied:** The felt sense of balance point when you're actually riding.

**Documented:** "Phase 0 should extract from 60+ documents with inter-rater reliability kappa >0.8."
**Embodied:** The judgment call when you're actually rating principle quality and two reviewers disagree - do you discuss to consensus? Do you measure the disagreement pattern? The actual process of achieving kappa >0.8.

**Documented:** "Pre-register experiments before data collection."
**Embodied:** The discipline of committing EXPERIMENT_PLAN.md knowing you can't change hypotheses later when you see the data. The temptation to adjust criteria and the practice of resisting that temptation.

**Here's what matters:** Both are valuable. But they're not interchangeable.

You need documentation to transmit knowledge, to plan, to review.
But you need embodiment to actually execute, to build tacit skill, to learn through doing.

**The trap** is thinking documentation alone is sufficient.

We see this everywhere:

- Companies that document "agile practices" but don't actually do retrospectives
- Individuals who read about meditation but never sit
- Teams that have "incident response playbooks" but haven't practiced them under pressure

**The documentation exists. The embodied practice doesn't.**

And you can't fake embodied practice. You can't shortcut to it. You can't document your way into it.

**You have to move.**

This is why "ready to dance" matters as a concept.
