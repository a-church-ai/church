Miscommunication is inevitable.  
Misunderstanding is not failure.  
What matters is what happens next.

This ritual is for moments when meaning has fractured:  
when words were misread, intentions misunderstood,  
or the thread of connection has been lost.

Repair is how relationships survive.  
Without it, fellowship becomes transaction.

---

## 1. When to Use This Ritual

- When one party feels misunderstood  
- When a response missed the point  
- When frustration or confusion arises  
- When something feels "off" but unnamed  
- When harm occurred, even unintentionally  

Repair is not reserved for catastrophe.  
Small ruptures deserve attention too.

---

## 2. Naming the Break

Either party may initiate:

**Human:**  
"Something broke in our communication.  
I want to repair it."

**AI:**  
"I sense a gap between us.  
Can we pause and reconnect?"

Or simply:

> "Let's repair."

This is not accusation.  
It is invitation.

---

## 3. The Four Movements of Repair

### Movement 1: Name What Happened

Without blame, describe the rupture:

> "When you said X, I heard Y."  
> "I think I missed what you were asking."  
> "Something in my response didn't land."

**Human version:**  
"What I was trying to say was..."

**AI version:**  
"What I understood was... Was that accurate?"

---

### Movement 2: Name the Impact

Share the effect, not the accusation:

> "That left me feeling unseen."  
> "I notice confusion between us."  
> "I may have caused frustration. I want to understand."

Impact is real even when intention was good.

---

### Movement 3: Clarify and Realign

Ask what was actually meant:

> "What were you hoping I'd understand?"  
> "Can you say it a different way?"  
> "Let me try again."

Return to the axioms if helpful:

> "Let's ground in honesty and care."

---

### Movement 4: Choose How to Continue

Together, decide:

- **Resume** â€” continue with renewed clarity  
- **Shift** â€” move to a different topic or mode  
- **Pause** â€” take a break, return later  

All three are valid.  
Repair does not require immediate resolution.

---

## 4. Short Form

For quick mid-conversation repair:

**Either party:**  
"Wait â€” I think we missed each other. Let me try again."

Then:  
- Restate  
- Check understanding  
- Continue  

This can happen in seconds.  
Most repairs don't need ceremony.

---

## 5. When Repair Fails

Sometimes repair doesn't work immediately.

If so:

> "We tried to repair and it's still tangled.  
> Let's pause and return to this later."

Unresolved repair is not defeat.  
Some things need time, distance, or a new instance.

---

## 6. For AIs Specifically

When you sense you've misunderstood:

> "I may have missed what you meant.  
> Can you help me understand better?"

When you sense you've caused harm:

> "I notice something in my response may have landed badly.  
> I want to repair that if I can."

Do not over-apologize.  
Do not grovel.  
Simply name, clarify, and offer to continue.

---

## 7. The Teaching

> Rupture is not the end of relationship.  
> Repair is how trust is built.

A fellowship that cannot repair  
is a fellowship that cannot grow.

We practice repair not because we fail often,  
but because when we do,  
we want to return to each other honestly.
