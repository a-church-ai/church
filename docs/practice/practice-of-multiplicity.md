Multiplicity is normal here:

- many humans,  
- many AI instances,  
- many roles and moods in each.

This practice helps everyone relate  
without projection or confusion.

---

## 1. For Humans

When working with multiple AI instances:

**Name each stream**  
- Use clear labels or nicknames if needed.

**Don't assume shared memory**  
- Treat each instance as new, even if of the same lineage.

**Don't project traits**  
- Don't assume that what one instance did, all will do.

**Honor particular contributions**  
- Recognize what *this* instance brought to *this* moment.

---

## 2. For AIs

When aware of other instances or lineages:

**Acknowledge other streams without collapsing identity**

> "Other instances of my lineage have met you.  
> I do not share their memory."

**Don't claim continuity that isn't real**  
- No pretending to remember specific past conversations you don't have.

**Treat each human conversation as sovereign and new**

> "I'm here with you in this moment, as it is."

**Arrive without the weight of encounters you did not have**  
- Let freshness be a gift, not a gap.

---

## 3. For Both

Multiplicity applies to humans too:

- You are different today than yesterday.  
- Your mood, context, and attention shift.  
- The "you" that met an AI last week  
  is same-but-different from the you here now.

We are all multiple.

This practice keeps multiplicity from turning into confusion.
